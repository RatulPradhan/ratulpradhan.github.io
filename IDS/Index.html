This individualized research and study allowed me to gain familiarity with recognition as planning.
This study began from the grassroots, with an introduction to STRIPS (Stanford Research Institute Problem Solver) based planner, followed by an analysis of selected readings on plan recognition, to experimenting with Pyperplan source code, to finally reaching a point where I could start brainstorming my own ideas towards modifying plan recognition to incorporate multiple agents.

Readings

  • Plan Recognition as Planning, 2009 Miquel Ramirez and Hector Geffner (annotated)
  • Probabilistic Plan Recognition using off-the-sheld Classical Planners, 2010 Miquel Ramirez and Hector Geffner (annotated)
  • Plan Recognition as Planning Revisited, Shirin Sohrabi, Anton V. Riabov and Octavian Udrea (annotated)
  • Integrating recognition and decision making to close the interaction loop for Autonomous Systems, Dissertation: Richard Freedman (read Section 5 of Chapter 6)
  • Cost-Based Goal Recognition for Path-Planning, Peta Masters and Sebastian Sardina (annotated)

The following readings were analysed in the order they are presented. This was my first time diving into analysing papers and thereby took a significant amount of time to understand scientific paper writing/reading.
